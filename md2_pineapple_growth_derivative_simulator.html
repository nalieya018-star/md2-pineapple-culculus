The MD2 Maturation Problem

In agro-industrial processing, harvesting the MD2 Pineapple at its peak physicochemical phase is vital. Early harvest leads to high acidity and poor sugar content (low Brix), while late harvest causes internal browning and yeast spoilage.

During the final weeks after flower induction (forcing), the fruit enters a rapid growth phase modeled by a biological Logistic Growth Curve.

The derivative $f'(x)$ represents the instantaneous rate of fruit growth (g/week). Tracking this derivative allows food scientists and farmers to determine when mass acceleration slows down, indicating that the plant has shifted its metabolism from size growth to sugar synthesis—the optimal harvest cue!

Mathematical Kinetics

1. Logistic Mass Function $f(x)$
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$

Yields average fruit weight (g) where $L$ is carrying capacity ($1850\text{g}$), $k$ is growth constant ($0.45$), and $x_0$ is the inflection midpoint ($15.5\text{ weeks}$).

2. Growth Rate Derivative $f'(x)$
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}\right)^2}$$

Calculated using the quotient and chain rules. Yields the instantaneous growth rate in grams per week.
